What are Margins?

Margins are the outmost space used in CSS. Margins are used to create space around elements/content, outside of borders. You can individually change the properties for each side of the margin for an element. e.g. the top, right, bottom, and left borders which can look like this when changed:

this is a 20% margin from the top
while this is a 50% margin from the left

What are Borders

Borders are the inbetween the margin and padding. Borders again go around elements to create a border sort of style. They can have a few different styles such as solid, dotted or dashed (among others) you can also define the width of borders such as this:

this border is 10px wide

an example of code for this is:

#thin-border{
border-width: 10px;
border-style: solid; }

this border is 20px wide

What is Padding?

Padding is the inner-most space that you are able to manipulate around content/elements. Padding is very similar to the margin with the main difference being simply that padding is the innermost space inside the element while the margin is the outermost (refer to picture below).
